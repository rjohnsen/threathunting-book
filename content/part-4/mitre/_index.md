MITRE ATT&CK gives us a shared vocabulary for adversary behaviour. These notes use that vocabulary as an investigation aid, not as a verdict, severity score, or substitute for evidence.

A technique label becomes useful when it helps you ask better questions: What behaviour was observed? Which identities, processes, systems, and data sources support the mapping? What happened immediately before and after? Is the behaviour expected in this context?

{{% notice style="warning" title="Technique mappings are hypotheses" %}}
The same command, protocol, or event can support several techniques, or none at all. Map behaviour only after you understand the surrounding activity, and record the evidence behind the mapping.
{{% /notice %}}
