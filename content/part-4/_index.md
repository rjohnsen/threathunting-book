Field notes are working references: structured for practical use during an investigation, while remaining explicit about assumptions, telemetry gaps, and the pivots that turn an observation into evidence.

{{% notice style="info" title="How to use these notes" %}}
Treat examples as starting points, not universal detections. Validate field names against your own schema, establish what is normal in your environment, and preserve the evidence that supports or weakens your hypothesis.
