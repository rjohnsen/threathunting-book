## How to read Huntbook

Huntbook is not a traditional documentation site and not a linear tutorial. It is a practitioner’s field manual, written from experience, and structured around **how defensive systems actually behave under pressure**. If you approach it as a collection of techniques, queries, or tools, you will miss the point.

---

## The lens used throughout this book

Everything in Huntbook is written through the lens of **Threat-Informed Defense (TID)**. This means:

- threats are used to **prioritize and validate**, not to create fear
- intelligence is something you both **consume and produce**
- defense is treated as a system with feedback loops, not a checklist of controls

Threat hunting, detection engineering, incident response, and tooling are discussed as **functions within that system**, not as isolated practices.

---

## On structure and intent

The structure of Huntbook is intentional. Sections that appear "practical" are supported by sections that explain **why those activities matter — and when they do not**.  Foundational topics are placed before execution on purpose.

Skipping context and jumping straight to techniques may be convenient, but it often leads to false confidence. Many defensive failures begin exactly there.

---

## On products and omissions

Huntbook does not aim to promote, compare, or comprehensively document security products. If information about specific SIEMs, SOAR platforms, EDRs, or commercial tooling is missing, that omission is intentional. Product capabilities change frequently, marketing language distorts understanding, and tool-specific knowledge ages poorly.

The focus of this book is the **handicraft of defensive work**: _how practitioners think, validate assumptions, reason about telemetry, and correct systems over time._

Tools are treated as interchangeable instruments. The craft — not the brand — is what endures.

---

## What this book assumes

Huntbook assumes that:
- defensive work is constrained by real organizational, technical, and human limits
- most problems attributed to analysts are in fact **structural**
- visibility, ownership, and feedback matter more than tooling choice

Not all environments are ready for advanced threat hunting. Recognizing that is a form of maturity, not a failure.

---

## How this book evolves

Huntbook is a living work. Some sections are refined essays. Others are closer to field notes. Content is added, reframed, and occasionally retired as understanding improves. Clarity is preferred over completeness.

If you are looking for universal answers or polished guarantees, this is not the right place.  If you are interested in understanding why things break — and how to build defenses that can be corrected over time — continue with the **Threat-Informed Defense** section.
