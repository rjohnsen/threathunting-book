## Roger Johnsen

Roger Johnsen is an information security professional focused on **Blue Team operations, threat hunting, and detection engineering**. His work centers on strengthening defensive capability through a combination of analyst training, structured threat-informed approaches, and hands-on software development.

His professional focus spans threat hunting methodology, detection quality, and the practical realities of operating Security Operations Centers (SOCs). He places particular emphasis on building capabilities that scale through clear processes, verifiable data, and tooling that analysts actually understand and trust.

Roger maintains a strong hands-on profile and actively participates in Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges, including the SANS Holiday Hack Challenge and Hack The Box. In the 2021 SANS Holiday Hack Challenge, he was awarded runner-up for **Best Technical Answer**, receiving a challenge coin in recognition of the depth and quality of his work. Knowledge gained through these activities is continuously fed back into his professional practice.

---

## Professional background

Roger has a background in electronics, electrical engineering, and systems development, with an early focus on programming. He has been actively involved with open-source software since the late 1990s, primarily within the Linux and FreeBSD ecosystems.

Since 2005, his professional career has been concentrated on systems development and information security, with particular weight on operational security, threat hunting, and mentoring analysts. His experience spans consultancy, SOC build-up, managed security services, and internal enterprise security leadership.

## Predefender

Predefender is an independent initiative founded by Roger Johnsen in 2024, focused on **practical threat hunting and defensive capability building**.

Its primary output, the *Predefender Threat Hunt Book (Huntbook)*, serves as a continuously maintained knowledge base aimed at SOC analysts, threat hunters, and Blue Team practitioners. The material emphasizes methodology over tooling, and verification over assumptions.

Predefender’s guiding principle is that effective threat hunting emerges at the intersection of:

* detection engineering
* programming competence
* operational experience

The goal is not volume, automation theatre, or vendor abstraction — but **clarity, depth, and defensive relevance**.
